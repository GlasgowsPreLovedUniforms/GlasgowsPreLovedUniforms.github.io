About us

About us

Glasgow's Pre-loved Uniforms provides school uniform to all families who need it, ensuring that children and young people can wear warm school uniform, shoes and jackets regardless of their family’s financial situation.

We also encourage people who want to be more environmentally friendly to donate and/or reuse clothing instead of buying new.

Our Main Aims

  • To provide free pre-loved school uniform for all those who need it in the Glasgow area


  • To encourage recycling of school uniform and play our part in mitigating the climate emergency

Our Main Activities

  • Collecting pre-loved uniform via collection bins in local schools/community venues and at our premises

  • Laundering all items collected

  • Providing uniform in schools and fulfilling requests from social services to families in need

  • Providing uniform to asylum seeking & refugee children starting local school

  • Encouraging donations from those who want to recycle school uniform for environmental and/or financial reasons

  • Delivering uniform packs while maintaining social distancing

    How you can help

    Volunteering Opportunities

    Here at Glasgow’s Pre-loved Uniforms, we are always looking for a helping hand that will enable us to deliver our services to as many young people as possible.

    There are a number of roles that could be undertaken, from sorting clothes on rails to fundraising.

    To find out more about what you could do to help, send us an email or contact us via social media.

    Donating Uniforms

    Any school uniforms that you are able to donate would be greatly appreciated.

    If you would like to donate a pre-loved uniform to us you can do so by leaving them in a drop off location at one of our partner schools or community centres.

    Alternatively, you can drop them off to us directly, just send us a message via text, email or on our social media to arrange a drop off time and someone will meet you at our home at 321 Royston Road.

    Donations

    To ensure a great quality of service we have a number of running costs such as washing the uniforms, rental of storage spaces, delivery vehicle maintenance and fuel. Being a charity we rely on donations from the public and some grants to help cover all of these costs.

    Anything that you could donate would go a long way to help ensure that all children across Glasgow will have access to a school uniform.
